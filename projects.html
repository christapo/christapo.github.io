Algorithms for Phylogenetic Inference

Topics like community ecology, population biology, and the various -omics see an increasing demand for more efficient algorithms for reconstructing a phylogenetic tree as the number of taxa considered in these studies grow quite large. For example, microbial communities can often require millions of taxa to be considered.

I am working on implementing a variety of algorithms, heuristics, and metrics for phylogenetic inference in Python. Some of these Python implementations can be found in the bioinformatics package Scikit-bio.

Phylogenetic Networks and Biodiversity

Phylogenetic networks, in contrast to phylogenetic trees, are able to model reticulation events like hybridization horizontal gene transfer.

My research aims to understand the effect that including reticulation events has on measuring phylogenetic biodiversity as well as investigating algorithms for reconstructing phylogenetic networks.

Polytopes and Categorical Coherence

In category theory, especially higher category theory, the equivalence between strict and weak categorical strucutres is known as a categorical coherence problem. Equivalently, this is a problem of showing that certain types of diagrams commute.

Weak n-dimensional categories are defined with associativity conditions that have the shape of Stasheff's family of polytope, the associahedra. Similarly, functors between weak n-dimensional categories have functoriality conditions that have the shape of the multiplihedra, a related family of polytopes. Some research questions of mine are: If we continue and look at natural transformations between weak n-dimensional categories do these conditions also form polytopes? What about modifications, perturbations, and k-transfors in general? Can we utilize these polytopes for topological proofs of categorical coherence?

Cultural Phylogenetics and Rock Art Research

Writing systems, spoken languages, and even specific texts have been analyzed using methods from evolutionary biology, particularly phylogenetics. Examples of this can be seen in stemmatics from textual criticism to tree models used in historical linguistics.

I am interested in using phylogenetics to analyze the precursors of writing systems, namely petroglyphs and pictographs. Some interesting questions: Can a phylogenetic analysis of rock art be used to help us understand the interactions and cultural transmission between prehistoric cultural groups? Furthermore, can we interpret and use biodiversity measurements in this context?
